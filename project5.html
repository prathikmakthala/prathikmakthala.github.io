Health Tales: A Comparative ML Study

Predicting health outcomes using a Korean National Health Insurance dataset.

Project Overview

Health Tales is a comprehensive machine learning project that performs a comparative analysis of different classification models. The primary goal was to predict whether an individual is a drinker (`DRK_YN`) based on a wide range of health metrics.

The project involved a full data science workflow, including data cleaning, extensive exploratory data analysis (EDA), feature engineering, and a rigorous model evaluation process. The findings demonstrated the effectiveness of tree-based models, with Random Forest ultimately achieving the highest accuracy after hyperparameter tuning.

Key Features & Analysis Pipeline

  • **Dataset:** Leveraged a large dataset from the National Health Insurance Service in Korea, containing over 990,000 samples and 23 features.
  • **Comparative Modeling:** Trained and evaluated three different models: Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and K-Nearest Neighbors.
  • **Hyperparameter Tuning:** Employed GridSearchCV to systematically find the optimal parameters for each model, significantly boosting performance.
  • **Performance:** The tuned Random Forest and Decision Tree models achieved the highest accuracy at approximately 72%.
